Updating the website and recreating the blog

After a phase of consultation and research, I began design a one-page website for Cirro. It answered product questions and guided users to the product. I undertook the web development too – uncharacteristically installing my first VM and opening a shiney new GitHub account. Having direct access to the site allow us to make changes and adapt to client requests and user needs. The existing blog posts – written by a DB veteran – were rehoused in a new blog (Jekyll).

Print collateral

The business required materials like event banners, brochures, flyers and print templates. These were designed on-brand and contained a campaign message too. Banners and brochures had ideas and unique messaging. Concepts to capture attention and get people thinking about data security.

Cirro allowed me to flex my branding and communication muscles. We achieved a refined look, built a lead gen program and raised awareness about the product.
